# ICP — Who Alphaa Sells To

> v1.0. Beachhead: local health & home services, US + Canada. Secondary: bootstrapped SaaS founders. Buyer is always the **owner-operator, 35–60, non-technical, time-poor, buys outcomes not tools**.

## The common thread

They either **pay an SEO agency $1,000–$2,500/mo** and quietly suspect it's a scam, or they **do nothing** and quietly fear becoming invisible. Both feel the same trigger: *"People are asking ChatGPT now. Am I in the answer?"*

Budget context: our own spend is $2,000/mo (Google $1,200 / Meta $600 / $200 reserve), CAC target ≤ $300.

---

## Persona 1: Dr. Priya — Dentist / Med-spa owner

**Day in the life:** In the chair or supervising staff 9–6. Checks phone between patients. Reviews the practice's numbers Sunday night. Delegates marketing to an office manager or an agency she talks to once a quarter.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Pains** | Pays $1,500–$2,500/mo to an SEO/marketing agency; gets a PDF she doesn't read. New-patient flow is flat. A competitor practice keeps appearing "everywhere." |
| **Triggers** | A patient says "ChatGPT recommended you" (or worse, recommended the competitor). Just fired / is about to fire the agency at contract renewal. Read a dental-association newsletter piece about AI search. |
| **Where she is** | Facebook & Instagram (personally), dental Facebook groups (DentalTown adjacent), local chamber, dental conferences, Google when frustrated ("is my seo agency worth it"). |
| **Buys when** | She sees her own practice's scan score and her competitor named in an actual AI answer. Outcome framing: "more new patients," never "schema markup." |
| **LTV note** | High revenue per patient → tolerates Pro ($199) easily; multi-location groups = Pro. |

## Persona 2: Mike — HVAC / Plumbing company owner

**Day in the life:** In the truck or dispatching until 5. Does quotes at night. Answers his own phone. Marketing = Google Ads he half understands + a website his nephew built + maybe an agency he resents.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Pains** | Lead flow is seasonal and fragile. Paying $1,000–$1,500/mo to an agency "for SEO" with no idea what he gets. Hates PDFs, hates jargon, hates contracts (got burned by a 12-month retainer). |
| **Triggers** | Slow month. Saw a "how AI is changing local search" video on Facebook. His Google Business Profile got suspended once and he realized how dependent he is. |
| **Where he is** | Facebook (heavily), YouTube, HVAC/plumber owner Facebook groups, Service Nation / Nexstar type communities, Google searching "cheap seo agency" or "[city] hvac marketing". |
| **Buys when** | The math is shown plainly: $99 vs $1,500, no contract, cancel anytime. Free scan with HIS company name in the results seals it. |
| **LTV note** | Price-sensitive → Starter. But loyal for years if the phone rings. |

## Persona 3: Sarah — Solo/small-firm lawyer (PI, family, estate)

**Day in the life:** Billable hours all day. Marketing decisions at 9pm. Already spends real money — lawyers pay the highest CPCs on Google — so she's sophisticated about spend but cynical about vendors.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Pains** | Paying $2,000–$2,500/mo for "legal SEO" that mostly produces blog posts nobody reads. Terrified of the referral pipeline drying up as searches move to AI. Every legal-marketing vendor overpromises. |
| **Triggers** | Read an ABA Journal / legal-tech piece on AI search. A prospective client mentioned "I asked ChatGPT for a lawyer." Annual marketing-budget review. |
| **Where she is** | LinkedIn (actively), state bar events, lawyer subreddits/listservs, Avvo/Clio ecosystems, Google ("seo agency alternative for law firms"). |
| **Buys when** | Honesty. She's been burned by guarantees — our "no one can guarantee AI rankings, here's what we actually do" positioning is the differentiator, not a liability. |
| **LTV note** | Highest LTV; one client pays for a decade of Alphaa. Pro tier natural fit. |

## Secondary: Ben — bootstrapped SaaS founder

Technical-ish, DIY marketing, reads about AEO on X/HN. Finds us organically via the blog (`aeo-for-saas-b2b`, `how-to-create-llms-txt-file`) and /for/saas. Don't spend ad budget here; let content catch him. Converts fast, churns faster — fine as filler revenue, not the beachhead.

## Objections → counters

| Objection | Counter | Asset |
|---|---|---|
| "Is AEO even real, or snake oil?" | It's how AI engines pick answers — from public signals (content, schema, reviews, GBP, llms.txt). We show you exactly what each engine says about you today, free. | Blog: `/blog/is-aeo-real`; the scan itself |
| "Can you guarantee I'll show up on ChatGPT?" | No — and anyone who guarantees rankings is lying to you. We shape the public signals AI reads and show you the measured change. That honesty is why we exist. | Positioning; never waver on this |
| "I already pay an agency" | Agency: ~$24,000/yr, still invisible on AI. Alphaa: $1,188/yr. Save up to $22,800/yr — and no contract, so run us alongside them for a month and compare. | AgencySection math; blog: `/blog/aeo-vs-seo-why-agencies-fail` |
| "I don't have time to learn a tool" | It's an autopilot, not a tool. Connect Google once (2 min); posts, tracking, and reports run themselves. Weekly email is all you read. | Onboarding; weekly report email |
| "AI search is a fad" | 1B+ daily AI searches and growing. Also: everything we do improves classic Google too, so worst case you got better SEO for $99. | Hero stat; `what-is-answer-engine-optimization` |
| "Why so cheap? What's the catch?" | Software, not humans. Agencies bill for meetings; we bill for compute. 14-day free trial, no card, judge it yourself. | Pricing page FAQs |

## Disqualifiers — do NOT sell to

- Businesses with no website and no Google Business Profile (nothing to optimize yet).
- Enterprises / franchises with in-house SEO teams wanting API access, white-label, or SLAs.
- Anyone demanding guaranteed rankings or "#1 on ChatGPT" — refund-and-churn risk, reputational risk.
- Outside US/Canada (for now — GBP/review mechanics and ad geo are NA-tuned).
- Affiliate/SEO-bro resellers wanting to bulk-run scans.
